About

I attend a high school in the Columbus Metropolitan Region of Ohio. I also attend Columbus State Community College concurrently just to challenge myself. Outside of school, I am in the programming club. In this, we prepare over the year to attend an ACM competition at Baldwin Wallace University near Cleveland. This brings me a lot of excitement as we get to compete against high schools around Ohio and maybe learn something from them. Also, if you place well you get to earn prizes. I'm also a part of our tennis team. I have been playing tennis for 10 years probably, taking classes at a few clubs around the area and playing with my dad. Our school tennis team puts a lot of effort in, and we never miss a practice during the season even with bad weather, so it is tough, but I still have fun with it. During summertimes we travel a lot to India, as that is where almost all of my family lives. Most of them are near a city called Bangalore in the Southern part of India. At home we speak Havyak-Kannada, which is a dialect of the language Kannada used commonly in Karnataka (the state that Bangalore is in). Sometimes I feel a bit disconnected from the culture, like in this story, but when I go to visit my cousins and everyone there it feels so nice. I got into programming from just having a computer for a long time and I gained interest from there. My parents are also in IT but I never really see what kind of work they do and I believe it is more project management rather than coding. From then, I have mostly been learning languages and concepts in the summertime, when I am out of school. This has mainly included C++ and Java. I enjoy C++ a lot more because you can tinker with some of the low level stuff, while also having access to higher level stuff like Java. Also the structure just seems a lot better than Java, where everything is an object which leads to messy code sometimes. I do understand though, that Java has lots of use cases in enterprise software which is why it was taught to me in AP Computer Science A, as well as self-studying. Also, C++ may be under fire from a new programming language from Google aiming to be a better version of it, although I don't see it happening until very far down the line, because right now basically everything has some C++ behind it. I also am trying to learn SQL, which I believe will be especially useful in Oracle-heavy jobs, or even if not that, just to expand my knowledge in computer science. This is occurring in my Database Systems Programs class, and by myself.
